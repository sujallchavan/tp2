History

History of Prashala

Swatantrya Sainik Raykumar B Gujar Secondary and Higher Secondary Prashala, including its junior college, was founded upon the noble ideals of Maharashtra’s foremost social reformers—Mahatma Jyotiba Phule, Savitribai Phule, Rajarshi Shahu Maharaj, Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar, Sayajirao Gaikwad, Karmaveer Bhaurao Patil, and Maharshi Dhondo Keshav Karve. These leaders were pioneers in advocating for equality through education, envisioning a society where knowledge would be the right of every individual, irrespective of their social or economic background. Their relentless efforts helped bring education to the masses, challenging the barriers of caste, gender, and poverty.

Inspired by their vision, our founders, Sahebrao Shankarrao Dhamdhere (known as Anna) and freedom fighter Raikumar Bhogilal Gujar (known as Dadaji), took upon themselves the mission of spreading education in Talegaon Dhamdhere and the neighboring villages. In 1952, they established the Shikshan Prasarak Mandal, a trust dedicated to delivering quality education to underserved communities. With "Tamaso Ma Jyotirgamaya" or "Lead me from darkness to light" as their guiding motto, Anna and Dadaji committed their lives to creating a school that would uplift the poorest of the poor, bringing the light of learning to all.

Overcoming Challenges in Early Years

In its formative years, Shikshan Prasarak Mandal overcame significant obstacles. Shirur, being a drought-prone region with limited educational resources, posed many challenges for a new institution. However, the determination of Anna and Dadaji enabled the school to grow steadily. Early supporters like Colonel Sadashivrao Phadnis, who generously offered his land at a low cost, provided the much-needed foundation for the institute’s expansion. As enrollment rose, the original structure could no longer accommodate the number of students. Recognizing this need, Shikshan Prasarak Mandal requested more land from the Government of Maharashtra. Chief Minister Barrister A. R. Antule, acknowledging their dedication, granted a 9-acre plot, which allowed the institute to expand and better serve the community, aided by the advocacy of Headmaster L.D. Sonawane.

Following this acquisition, construction began on a new, well-equipped 22-classroom facility. Through the dedication of leaders like Chairman Raikumar Bhogilal Gujar, Secretary Sahebrao Shankarrao Dhamdhere, and several other committed individuals, the building was completed and inaugurated by Defense Minister Sharad Chandraji Pawar in 1991. Contributors such as Dattartray Tatyaba Dhamdhere, Sampatrao Bhagwantrao Dhamdhere, Jaykumar Punnchand Runwal, and others made invaluable efforts to bring this vision to life. This achievement symbolized the institute’s commitment to quality education and became a major milestone, creating an environment where students from Talegaon Dhamdhere and surrounding villages could learn in a supportive, resourceful setting.

A Legacy of Empowerment Through Education

Today, Swatantrya Sainik Raykumar B Gujar Secondary and Higher Secondary Prashala, along with its junior college, continues to fulfill the mission of its founders by providing accessible, quality education. The institution has become a center of learning for the community, where students from secondary through junior college receive the guidance and resources to pursue their dreams. Embracing the motto "Tamaso Ma Jyotirgamaya," our school remains dedicated to lighting the path of knowledge and empowerment for every student, ensuring they are prepared for a bright and impactful future. This lasting legacy of learning affirms that with commitment and community support, education can indeed transform lives, lifting entire communities from darkness into light.
